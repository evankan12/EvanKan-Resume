In 1975, my grandparents opened up a restaurant called the Castro Valley Hofbrau, where my father helped out from time to time when he was in high school. The dishes from the restaurant that my father makes today inspired me to delve into the culinary arts. Growing up, my father taught me how to cook, but my goal was to make my own recipes and try to create food fusions entirely novel. The first successful dish I ever made was a humble bowl of fried rice. The simplicity of this dish filled me with joy, but an ambitious side of me yearned to add my own touch to the popular dish. I soon learned that my dishes were a canvas with which I could express my creativity. I was able to showcase my culinary innovation through my curiosity of combining ingredients together and producing flavors that were distinctly my own. Slowly, my original fried rice recipe transformed into many different recipes all with a variety of flavors and aromas. It evolved from simple, salt and pepper fried rice into honey mustard glaze on salmon with green onion and scrambled egg on rice. I never thought my desire to perfect a bowl of fried rice would teach me to harness my creativity and impact my work in the future. My involvement in culinary arts allows me to think outside the box and increases my problem-solving skills by doing research beforehand and coming up with a solution with the knowledge I gained. For example, I can make a dish with few ingredients with a short amount of time to complete the task. I can transfer these skills into other domains like presenting an out-of-the-box business pitch or create an eye-catching shoe-selling platform within a short amount of time. No report card can depict my limitless creativity, which I am confident will help me find success. If there is one lesson my culinary skills have taught me, it's that I can always improve on myself, and create versions of my work that surpass the last.
